Principal investigator

Javier Duarte is an Assistant Professor of Physics at UC San Diego and a member of the CMS experiment at the CERN Large Hadron Collider. He leads a research group developing new artificial intelligence (AI) techniques for high-energy particle collisions to better measure the properties and interactions of elementary particles, like the Higgs boson, and search for new physics. Before joining UC San Diego, he was a Lederman postdoctoral fellow at Fermilab and received his Ph.D. in Physics at Caltech and his B.S. in Physics and Mathematics at MIT. Prof. Duarte has received a Sloan Research Fellowship, RCSA Cottrell Scholar Award, DOE Early Career Award, and is a co-PI of the NSF HDR Institute for Accelerated AI Algorithms for Data-Driven Discovery.

Research directions

Our lab is interested in:

  • LHC data analysis for Higgs boson and exotic new physics
  • Hardware-accelerated machine learning for trigger and computing
  • Geometric deep learning for particle physics
  • Diversity, inclusion, and social justice in physics
